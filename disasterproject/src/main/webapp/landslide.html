Landslides

A landslide is the movement of rock, soil, or debris down a slope, often triggered by factors such as heavy rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, or human activities like deforestation or construction. It can occur suddenly or gradually, resulting in the mass movement of material downslope. Landslides can range in size from small, localized events to large-scale disasters, causing damage to property, infrastructure, and posing risks to human lives and the environment.

Do's & Don'ts

Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind during a landslide:

Do's Don'ts
1. Stay Informed: Monitor weather reports and landslide warnings.
2. Move to Safe Areas: If you live on a slope, consider relocating during heavy rain.
3. Keep Emergency Supplies Ready: Have a kit with essentials like food, water, and first aid.
4. Report Early Signs: Inform authorities of cracks or unusual ground movements.
5. Reinforce Slopes: Take steps to prevent erosion near your home if possible.
6. Follow Evacuation Orders: Leave immediately if authorities advise evacuation. 		1. Don’t Build on Steep Slopes: Avoid construction in landslide-prone areas.
2. Don’t Block Drains: Blocked drains increase landslide risk by preventing proper water flow.
3. Avoid Removing Vegetation: Trees and plants help stabilize the soil.
4. Don’t Use Heavy Machinery on Slopes: This can destabilize the soil, especially during rain.
5. Avoid Ignoring Warning Signs: Cracks, tilting trees, and unusual sounds may indicate landslide risk.
6. Don’t Return Until Cleared: Avoid returning to affected areas until officials declare it safe.

By following these do's and don'ts, you can help minimize the risks associated with landslides and stay safe during such natural disasters.
