Cyclones

A cyclone is a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure. Cyclones are characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, and often cause extensive damage, especially along coastal regions. They form over warm ocean waters and can bring about destructive winds, storm surges, and flooding. Cyclones are classified by their intensity, which may range from tropical depressions to severe tropical cyclones, with the potential to cause significant destruction to life, property, and the environment.

Do's & Don'ts

Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind during a cyclone:

Do's Don'ts
1. Stay Informed: Regularly check weather updates from reliable sources like meteorological departments.
2. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Have an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, first aid, flashlights, and batteries.
3. Secure Your Home: Reinforce doors and windows, and bring outdoor objects indoors.
4. Evacuate if Advised: Follow local authority guidelines and evacuate to designated shelters when instructed.
5. Charge Devices: Keep mobile phones and other communication devices charged for emergency contact.
6. Keep Important Documents Safe: Store documents in waterproof bags or containers. 		1. Don't Ignore Warnings: Never underestimate cyclone warnings; they are based on detailed forecasts and can save lives.
2. Avoid Coastal Areas: Stay away from beaches, rivers, and coastal zones to avoid high tides, storm surges, and flooding.
3. Don’t Use Electrical Appliances: Avoid using electrical devices to prevent short circuits and electric shocks during the storm.
4. Do Not Drive in Flooded Areas: Roads may be damaged or submerged; driving in these conditions can be hazardous.
5. Avoid Panic Buying: Purchase only essential items to ensure adequate supplies for everyone in the community.
6. Don’t Spread or Believe Rumors: Avoid sharing unverified information or rumors, as it can lead to panic and confusion. Rely only on official sources for updates and instructions.

By following these do's and don'ts, you can help ensure your safety and the safety of your loved ones during a cyclone.
