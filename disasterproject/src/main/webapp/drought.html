Droughts

Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low precipitation levels, leading to water scarcity and dry conditions in an area. It can result from natural climatic variability, such as below-average rainfall or snowfall, as well as human activities such as overuse of water resources, deforestation, and climate change. Droughts can have severe impacts on ecosystems, agriculture, water supplies, and socio-economic activities.

Do's & Don'ts

Do's and Don'ts During Drought:

Do's Don'ts
1. Conserve Water: Use water-saving techniques at home and in agriculture.
2. Store Rainwater: Implement rainwater harvesting methods.
3. Plant Drought-Resistant Crops: Opt for crops that require less water.
4. Educate the Community: Spread awareness about water conservation.
5. Use Water Wisely: Limit washing cars, watering gardens, and other non-essential uses.

2. Don’t Ignore Restrictions: Follow government water-use restrictions carefully.
3. Avoid Planting Water-Intensive Crops: During droughts, opt for crops that require less water.
4. Don’t Burn Waste: Burning can worsen air quality in dry conditions.
5. Avoid Excessive Groundwater Use: Excessive pumping can deplete local water tables.
6. Don’t Delay Repairs on Leaks: Fix any leaking taps or pipes to prevent water wastage.

By following these do's and don'ts, individuals, communities, and governments can contribute to mitigating the impacts of drought and promoting sustainable water management practices.
