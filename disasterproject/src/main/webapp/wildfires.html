Wildfires

Wildfires are large, uncontrolled fires that spread rapidly through vegetation and forested areas. They can be caused by natural factors like lightning or by human activities such as unattended campfires, discarded cigarettes, or deliberate arson. Wildfires pose significant risks to lives, property, and ecosystems, often resulting in the destruction of vast areas of land, loss of biodiversity, and air quality deterioration.

Do's & Don'ts

Do's and Don'ts During Wildfires:

Do's Don'ts
1. Stay Informed: Monitor local news and weather updates.
2. Create a Defensible Space: Clear dry vegetation around your property.
3. Keep Emergency Kits Ready: Include essentials like water, food, and medications.
4. Evacuate When Advised: Follow evacuation orders promptly.
5. Use Masks: Protect yourself from smoke inhalation.
6. Inform Authorities: Report any sightings of fire immediately. 		1. Don’t Leave Fires Unattended: Ensure campfires and barbeques are fully extinguished.
2. Don’t Throw Cigarette Butts: Dispose of them responsibly.
3. Avoid Burning Trash: This can easily ignite nearby vegetation.
4. Don’t Block Roads: Keep access routes clear for emergency vehicles.
5. Don’t Ignore Alerts: Always heed wildfire warnings.
6. Avoid Outdoor Activities: Refrain from activities that could generate sparks.

By adhering to these safety guidelines, communities can reduce the risk of wildfires and respond effectively during such emergencies.
