Earthquakes

An earthquake is a sudden and violent shaking of the Earth's surface, typically caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's crust. This movement releases energy in the form of seismic waves, leading to ground shaking and potentially causing damage to buildings, infrastructure, and landscapes. Earthquakes can vary in intensity and magnitude, with larger earthquakes capable of causing widespread destruction and loss of life.

Do's & Don'ts

Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind during an earthquake:

Do's Don'ts
1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Protect yourself from falling objects.
2. Stay Indoors if Inside: Stand under door frames or sturdy furniture.
3. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials like food, water, and first aid.
4. Secure Heavy Objects: Anchor furniture and other heavy items to walls.
5. Identify Safe Spots: Know safe areas in each room where you can shelter.
6. Listen to Emergency Broadcasts: Stay updated through radios or official apps. 		1. Don’t Run Outside During Shaking: Falling debris can be more dangerous than staying inside.
2. Avoid Using Elevators: Power outages may trap you inside.
3. Don’t Stand Near Windows or Glass: These can shatter and cause injuries.
4. Don’t Use Open Flames: Gas leaks may occur, which can ignite.
5. Avoid Parking Under Bridges: Stay clear of overpasses and bridges during or after an earthquake.
6. Don’t Panic: Try to stay calm and focused on safety procedures.

By following these do's and don'ts, you can help stay safe during an earthquake and reduce the risk of injuries or property damage.
