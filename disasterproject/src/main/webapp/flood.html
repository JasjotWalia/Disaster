Floods

A flood is an overflow of water onto normally dry land, often caused by heavy rainfall, melting snow, ice jams, or dam failures. Floods can also be triggered by coastal storm surges, tsunamis, or rapid accumulation of water from intense storms. The excess water inundates areas that are usually dry, leading to temporary or prolonged submersion of land, buildings, roads, and infrastructure. Floods can cause significant damage to property, pose risks to human lives, disrupt communities, and have environmental impacts on ecosystems.

Do's & Don'ts

Here are some do's and don'ts to keep in mind during a flood:

Do's Don'ts
1. Move to Higher Ground: Seek safety away from water levels.
2. Turn Off Utilities: Switch off gas, electricity, and water supply if safe to do so.
3. Keep Emergency Supplies Ready: Have essentials like drinking water, food, and medicines.
4. Avoid Flooded Roads: Use alternative routes or stay put if water levels are high.
5. Monitor Weather Alerts: Stay updated on flood warnings.

5. Don’t Leave Animals Unprotected: Secure pets in safe areas.
6. Don’t Return Home Prematurely: Wait for authorities to declare the area safe.

By following these do's and don'ts, you can help ensure your safety and the safety of others during a flood.
